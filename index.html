About Mary Ogbuka Kenneth

I am a Teaching Scholar in the Department of Computing at Imperial College London and a PhD Representative of my cohort. I am also a co-organizer of the Imperial Computing Conference (ICC). Additionally, I serve as the Marketing Coordinator of Women and Non-Binary Individuals in Computing (WNBIC).

Prior to joining Imperial College London, I completed my master's thesis at the Federal University of Technology, Minna, under the guidance of Dr. Sulaimon A. Bashir. Our research focused on using machine learning and computer vision to detect face morphing attacks in passport images. This project was honored with the Best Paper Award at ICTA 2020: the 3rd International Conference on Information and Communication Technology and Applications.

I obtained my MTech degree (with distinction) from the Federal University of Technology, Minna, Nigeria. My first research project, supervised by Dr. Ovre Jeffrey Agushaka at the Federal University of Lafia, focused on the separation of overlapping sickle cells in blood film images. I received my BSc (First Class) from the Federal University of Lafia in Nigeria.

My research interests lie in Multimodality, Natural Language Processing (NLP), Computer Vision, Audio Processing, and Data Analysis, with a focus on leveraging multimodal datasets for health improvement.
